BRIEF DESCRIPTION
This compound heterozygous variant was found to be absent from other unaffected family members and 400 ethnically-matched healthy control individuals. In addition, this compound variant was co-segregated with the RP phenotype in an autosomal recessive manner. In silico analysis revealed that both c.C958T (p.R320X) and c.G1355A (p.R452H) could compromise the protein function of CYP4V2. These results strongly suggest this compound variant to be a disease-causing variant, which expands upon the spectrum of currently known CYP4V2 genetic variants associated with retinal diseases. (Source [https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8972287/](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8972287/))
